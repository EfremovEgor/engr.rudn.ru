History of Academy

The 5th of February 1960

Creation of the University

Resolution of the Central Committee of the CPSU and the Council of Ministers of the USSR "On the organization Peoples' Friendship University", dated February 5, 1960, It informed that the "Peoples' Friendship University trains engineers, agricultural specialists, teachers, doctors, economists and specialists in other branches of knowledge."

The 17th of March 1960

The building on Ordzhonikidze

Resolution of the Central Committee of the CPSU and the Council of Ministers of the USSR "On the organization Peoples' Friendship University", dated February 5, 1960, It informed that the "Peoples' Friendship University trains engineers, agricultural specialists, teachers, doctors, economists and specialists in other branches of knowledge."

The 4th of June 1960

The first specialties of the faculty

Resolution of the Council of Ministers of the USSR No. 604 of June 14, 1960 “On events in connection with the organization of the Peoples' Friendship University”, in section 3, "Faculties and specialties", the first ones were identified specialties at the faculty are "Mechanical Engineering", "Construction", "Geology", "Development of mineral deposits", "Steam and gas turbines".

The 17th of February 1961

Foundation of the Faculty of Engineering

Work on the creation of the Faculty of Engineering, the compilation of educational materials plans and programs and recruitment were started in 1961, when on February 17, a graduate was appointed the first dean of the faculty Faculty of Mechanics and Technology of Moscow Higher Education Mikhail Sergeevich Shkalikov Bauman Technical College (1920 – 1996).

The 1th of September 1961

The first admission of students

93 students from the preparatory faculty were transferred to the 1st year of the Faculty of Engineering.

The 29th of June 1965

The first graduation from the Faculty of Engineering

The first graduate of the Faculty of Engineering was 61 people from 27 countries. 73% of first-year students received a the results of defending their graduation projects are excellent and good evaluations. Diplomas with honors were awarded to: Amelia Brito Rojas (Cuba), Jacqueline Volelle Brisson (Haiti), Francisco Cabot Costa (Uruguay), Baldev Krishan (India), Slava Ivanov (USSR) and Nikolai Gudimenko (USSR).

September 1, 1965 — September 1, 1972

Mikhail Petrovich Bochai – Dean of the Faculty

In 1965-1972, the faculty was headed by a candidate of technical Sciences, Associate Professor Mikhail Petrovich Bochai. He has been working at the University since February 1962 His work as dean and head of the department metal technologies was awarded the Order of Friendship of Peoples for achievements in the business of training highly qualified specialists for developing countries.

September 1, 1972 — September 1, 1981

Dmitry Vasilyevich Nesmeyanov – Dean of the Faculty

September 1, 1981 — September 1, 1989

Trofimov Nikolay Nikolaevich – Dean of the Faculty

Candidate of Geological and Mineralogical Sciences, Professor. to He has been working since August 1961. In 1975, on the occasion of the 15th anniversary on the anniversary of the University, he was awarded the Order of the Badge of Honor.
